FIG.00 The First Gift

1477

The First Ring

Archduke Maximilian of Austria commissions a diamond ring for Mary of Burgundy — the first recorded diamond engagement ring. A single gesture creates a cultural convention that outlasts the empire that commissioned it.

FIG.01 Rough Diamond

1867

First Contact

Eureka Diamond discovered in South Africa. The Kimberley Rush begins. Extraction formalized under colonial infrastructure. A global supply chain is born from a single stone in the ground.

FIG.02 Sorting Table

1888

Consolidation

De Beers Consolidated Mines founded. Supply control established. One entity begins pricing the world's diamonds. Scarcity, for the first time, is manufactured.

FIG.03 Forever

1948

The Campaign

A Diamond Is Forever campaign launches. Emotional value engineered at scale. Diamonds become the symbol of permanent commitment. Desire, not geology, sets the price from here.

FIG.04 Certificate

2003

Certification

Kimberley Process established. Conflict-free certification introduced. Traceability becomes a selling point. The chain of custody enters the conversation for the first time.

FIG.05 Lab Diamond

2015

The Disruption

CVD and HPHT lab-grown diamonds reach commercial scale. Physically identical to mined stones, certified by the same labs, indistinguishable without spectrography. The stone story collapses as a differentiator. The market splits: commodity vs. point of view.

FIG.06 Design Sketch

Now

The Designer Era

The supply chain is visible now. D2C ateliers source direct. The 4Cs are understood by the client. The stone story has been commoditized. What remains as the genuine differentiator — the thing that cannot be priced by weight or grade — is the designer's point of view. RarityX was founded on this position.

Setting Procedures

Step 01

Design Resolution

The brief closes. CAD begins. Every prong placement is argued before material cost is considered. Dimension changes stop here. The render is the last version of the piece in which everything is still possible.

Step 02

Wax Model and Casting

The wax carries every decision made in the brief. Investment casting translates CAD geometry into metal. The shrinkage rate is known. The outcome is not a surprise — it is a confirmation.

Step 03

Stone Selection

Three to five candidates reviewed under differing light conditions. GIA grade verified. Face-up diameter confirmed against setting tolerance to 0.1mm. The stone chosen is not the best stone available. It is the correct stone for this design.

Step 04

Metal Preparation

Setting seat cut to the exact girdle profile of the selected stone. Not the CAD profile. The actual stone. Prongs filed, contact points dressed. Preparation takes longer than setting.

Step 05

Setting

Stone seated. Prongs secured in opposing pairs, not in sequence. Tension distributed evenly. The stone does not move. No light leaks beneath the girdle. The setting is only complete when the loupe confirms both of these things simultaneously.

Step 06

Final Inspection and Delivery

10x loupe, certificate comparison, surface polish, photography. The piece is documented before it leaves. The client receives the piece, the GIA certificate, and a record of the design brief. That is the full delivery.
